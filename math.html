Course Details: Discrete Mathematics (CIT109)

Programme Information

Programme: B.Tech. (IT), 2nd Year

Semester: 4

Credits: 4

Teaching Hours: 45(L) + 15(T) = 60 hrs

Total Marks: 100 (CA: 40, ESE: 60)

Duration of ESE: 3 hours

Course Type: Core Course

Prerequisites: BSC102, BSC104

Course Outcomes

  • Develop logical thinking and its application to computer science.
  • Reason mathematically about data types and structures in algorithms.
  • Apply counting principles, permutations, combinations, recurrence relations.
  • Illustrate graph properties and algorithms for real-world problems.
  • Analyze algebraic structures for engineering problems.
  • Use discrete structures in interdisciplinary problem solving.

Units

Unit 1: Fundamentals of Sets, Relations and Functions (10 hrs)

  • Operations on sets, Subsets, Types of sets
  • Ordered pairs, Proofs of identities, Partitions
  • Countable and uncountable sets, Inclusion-exclusion principle
  • Relations: Properties, Types, Composition, Closure
  • Equivalence, Compatibility, Partial order relations
  • Functions: Types, Sum/product, Hashing, Recursive functions

Unit 2: Propositional and Predicate Logic (3 hrs)

  • Propositional logic, Truth tables
  • Normal forms (CNF, DNF)
  • Validity of formulas, Inference rules
  • Predicate logic, Universal & Existential quantifiers

Unit 3: Combinatorial Mathematics (10 hrs)

  • Counting principles
  • Permutations and combinations
  • Pigeonhole principle
  • Recurrence relations (homogeneous & non-homogeneous)
  • Generating functions

Unit 4: Graph Theory (12 hrs)

  • Graph terminology, Directed/Undirected graphs
  • Bi-connected components, Articulation points
  • Eulerian chains/cycles, Planar graphs
  • Euler’s theorem, Isomorphic & homomorphic graphs
  • Hamiltonian path, Trees, Traversals, Spanning trees
  • Graph representation of relations, Applications

Unit 5: Algebraic Structures (10 hrs)

  • Group, Semigroup, Monoids
  • Homomorphism, Congruencies, Fields
  • Cyclic groups, Cosets, Normal subgroups
  • Dihedral & Permutation groups
  • Rings: Abelian, Unity, Inverse, Subrings
  • Ring homomorphism, Applications
